About Me

I'm a person with a variety of interests. I studied English in undergrad and had a blast reading great books and analyzing complex writing. After completing my degree, I went back to school to study Education, and eventually landed a job as high school English teacher. Although I found the work meaningful and my students interesting, I reached the conclusion that my personality wasn't suited to the constant performance that teaching required.


About a year ago, I knew I wouldn't be teaching another year, so I began exploring programming as a skill to boost my resume. I quickly realized how fun and interesting working with code can be, and dove head-first into learning the skills I would need to land a job as an entry-level web developer. Although this career trajectory might seem strange, for me it makes sense: in everything I do, I enjoy learning and solving complex problems. That is true in English, teaching, and in programming.


Outside of my professional interests, I am a frequent hiker and have been spending a lot of time walking the forests and sand dunes of West Michigan. I also have a musical side: I play the alto saxophone, and occasionally return to my efforts to learn the guitar. As an employee, I am a team player, highly organized, and driven to contribute to my team in any way I can. I work best on my own, so I have room to explore ideas freely, but I also understand the value of working in a team and finding solutions together and staying on the same page.


In searching for a job as a web developer, I am looking for a position that allows me to constantly learn, explore satisfying solutions to complex problems, and to work with a team that supports my growth.
